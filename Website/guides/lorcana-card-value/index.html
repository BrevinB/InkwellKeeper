How Much Are Your Lorcana Cards Worth?

Most Lorcana cards are worth pennies — and a few are worth hundreds of dollars. Knowing which is which is the difference between a tidy binder and money left on the table. Here's what actually drives card value, and how to price your whole collection without looking anything up.

The four things that set a card's price

1. Rarity and variant

Lorcana's rarity ladder runs Common → Uncommon → Rare → Super Rare → Legendary, with Enchanted alternate-art cards (plus Epic and Iconic variants in newer sets) sitting above all of them. Foil versions typically carry a premium over regular printings, and Enchanted cards are the chase — routinely the most valuable cards in every set.

2. Playability

Cards that anchor competitive decks hold value even at lower rarities. When a card shows up in tournament-winning lists, demand — and price — follows. Rotation matters too: cards legal in the current Core format command more than rotated ones.

3. Character popularity

Disney fandom is its own price engine. Elsa, Stitch, Mickey, and other fan-favorite characters outperform similar cards featuring less beloved characters, especially in Enchanted printings that collectors want on display.

4. Condition

Market prices assume Near Mint. Clean edges, sharp corners, good centering, and a scratch-free surface all matter — a played copy can be worth half of a mint one, while graded gem-mint copies of chase cards go the other direction.

How to price your collection (without a spreadsheet)

When to check your values

Prices move around set releases, ban announcements, and tournament results. Because pricing is live, your collection total tracks the market — check it before a trade night, after a big pull, or when a new set shifts the meta.
